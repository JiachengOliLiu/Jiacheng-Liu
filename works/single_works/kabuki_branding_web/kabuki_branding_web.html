ANU Za Kabuki Visual Identity & Website

ANU Za Kabuki Club is a student organisation at ANU, performing stylised Japanese classical theatre every year. I collaborated with the club executives to promote the club by polishing its old visual identity and building a new website.

See Za Kabuki website!

Logo Polish

-Shape/Motif-

Fish is not only one of Japanese people’s favourite food, but also a symbolic creature which often appears in Japanese traditional paintings and theatre plays. It has been the motif of the club’s old logo, which was designed in 1999. Due to its long history and cultural implication, I kept it as the main motif, and polished its shape in details, making it more concise and clear.

-Fonts-

For the Japanese logo font, I use a typeface called 勘亭流 (Kanteiryu), which is uniquely designed for kabuki. And for the English logo font, I use ‘Kids Rock’ to show the cute and vigorous characteristic of the club.

-Flexible Logo Versions-

I designed several flexible versions of the logo, in order to adapt it to different medias, backgrounds and compositions.

Decorating Patterns

-Tradition and Vigour-

Inspired by the red lanterns hanged on traditional kabuki theatre buildings in Japan, I designed the red background as a basic decoration, which also looks like a curtain. Also, I added some masks showing some typical make-ups of different kabuki characters to convey a sense of theatre. There are a few versions of the decorations which could be used in different circumstances.

Website

Our target audience of the website are mainly event organisers who might potentially invite the club to give visits and trips, and people who might be interested at going to our annual performances or joining the club. So I made a concise static site to showcase our performances and trips, as well as the information of recent events.
