Educational Webpage for The Arboretum

As a part of my Arboretum project (see The National Arboretum Canberra Branding project), I designed an interactive webpage for educational purpose targeting teenagers. The page presents an introduction to the history of Ginkgo Biloba, which is an important endangered tree planted in The Arboretum. This page is designed to be displayed on touch screens in galleries or exhibitions of The Arboretum venue. Through the interactions, it aims to raise young students’ interests and awareness of plant conservation. I used Adobe Illustrator to design the interface and did html, CSS and javascript coding to realise the production.

See the page! (Please open with Chrome or Safari)

Ideas


-Timeline-

The page is basically constructed with a timeline guiding 13 events, clearly presenting the essence of Ginkgo Biloba’s story. In order to relate the page to The Arboretum specifically, I added a horizontal timeline in the bottom of the page telling the tree’s story in Canberra especially in The Arboretum venue.

-Visual Elements-

The colours and graphics show coherence to the visual identity I designed for The Arboretum, and I applied more curve lines to present the shapes more clearly and make them look softer for my target audience. I designed the graphics in Adobe Illustrator and exported them as SVG pictures, which present a high visual quality but occupy little digital space. With the graphics, I created animated interactions using CSS and jQuery, in order to make the information more impressing and involve the audience’s engagement.

-User-friendly-

Considering teenagers as my target users, I try to make the texts friendly, concise and understandable. For people who are interested and want more detailed information about Ginkgo Biloba, I give external links to my original source of the texts.
