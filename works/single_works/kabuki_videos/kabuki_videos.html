ANU Za Kabuki Mirco Documentaries

In my post-grad years in ANU, I participated ANU Za Kabuki Club, a student organisation where we give a stylised Japanese classical stage play every year. We found that few people in Australia know about kabuki, and most students only get confused when they hear about our club, which is why we decided to make two micro documentaries to introduce and promote this art form and our club. One of the videos is about our actors and the other is about our backstage people. Through the two videos, we aim to present our experiences in the club and encourage more people to join us.

Opening Animation

-Tradition, Vigour and Vividness-

In order to catch viewers’ attention in the first glance, I polished our club logo and made a short opening animation.

-Logo Polish-

The old logo of Za Kabuki Club looks a little loose and unreadable, and the logo font doesn’t look so clear, so I refined its details and make it more concise and clear.

See ANU Za Kabuki Visual Identity & Website project

-Decorating Graphics-

Inspired by the red lanterns hanged on traditional kabuki theatre buildings in Japan, I designed the red background as a basic decoration, which also looks like a curtain. Also, I added some masks showing some typical make-ups of different kabuki characters to convey a sense of theatre.

-Motions-

I animated the graphics to make them look vivid, showing the vigorous and active characteristic of our club, also making our identity more recognisable.

Video Shooting & Interviews

-Getting Raw Materials-

In order to get the raw materials for editing, I did two preparations. Firstly, I did interviews with our main ‘characters’ - the actors and backstage people who talked in the videos. I asked a series of questions relating to their experiences in the club, so that they could tell what is fun to be a member of our club. Secondly, I took a lot of candid videos of our activities with the members’ permission. In this situation, I could catch the most natural scenes in which members enjoy themselves.

-Editing with a Logical Storyline-

With all the raw materials gotten, I sorted out a logical storyline to introduce and promote our experience with the club, and then edited the videos with the help from other club members.
