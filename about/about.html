Introduction

This is Jiacheng Liu who is majoring in Master of Design in The Australian National University and will graduate recently.

When I did my bachelor’s degree in Tongji University, I concentrated on graphic design and gained my abilities in branding design, layout design, UIX design and service design. During my time in ANU, I have expanded my scope to digital media, doing various projects like front-end web developing, animation, photography and videography. Through over 5 years of study and work, I have developed a human-centred approach in design-thinking and have also gained a strong sense of aesthetics. Also, in any new project, I always pay great attention to the research of the cultural context and relative knowledge. The multi-disciplinary scope of my studies demonstrates my ability to absorb new knowledge and adapt to different circumstances.

I am a curious person who holds diverse interests and is always eager to do something new. I enjoy my free time exploring different subjects such as classical music, modern art, mystery fictions, video games, philosophy and politics. These hobbies not only enrich my personal life, but also help me develop a better understanding of the world and enlighten my creativity.

For both my profession and life, I believe the most important spirits are innovation and imagination. My favourite Japanese author Yusuke Kishi noted in his dystopian novel, From the New World, that, 想像力こそが、全てを変える (Imagination is what changes everything). Just like what it says, imagination is something that could make a difference for anything. In Japanese, 想像力(imagination) and 創造力(innovation) share the same pronunciation, and I believe the two conceptions contribute to each other to a great extent. They both indicate the ability of creating new possibilities, with which we can make life much more colourful for ourselves, and make the world a better place for other people.
